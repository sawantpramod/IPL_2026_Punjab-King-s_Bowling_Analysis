# IPL_2026_Punjab-King-s_Bowling_Analysis
Punjab Kings entered IPL 2026 with one of the strongest batting line-ups on paper. Shreyas Iyer, Priyansh Arya, Cooper Connolly and Prabhsimran Singh formed a formidable top order that regularly posted competitive totals. Yet, the team's campaign was ultimately defined not by their batting prowess but by what happened when they took the field with the ball.
Some defeats were caused by expensive powerplays, where opposition openers raced to fifty inside the first six overs. Others slipped away because Punjab failed to take wickets at crucial moments, allowing partnerships to flourish unchallenged. The question is whether these were isolated incidents or recurring patterns that could be identified and addressed.
Instead of relying on match highlights or subjective opinions, this study uses ball-by-ball data from every Punjab Kings match in IPL 2026. Statistical techniques such as Welch's t-test and Cohen's d are applied to determine whether differences between wins and losses were genuine performance gaps or simply due to random variation.
The objective is straightforward:
Identify the strengths, weaknesses, and auction priorities for Punjab Kings ahead of IPL 2027.
Setting the Stage: The Three Phases of T20 Bowling
Bowling performance changes dramatically across different stages of a T20 innings. Each phase demands distinct skills and presents unique challenges:
Powerplay (Overs 1–6): Rewards attacking fast bowlers who can extract swing and bounce with the new ball. Teams aim to take early wickets while accepting the risk of boundaries.
Middle Overs (Overs 7–15): Tests control and spin. This is where quality wrist-spinners and economical medium-pacers build pressure through dot balls and tight lines.
Death Overs (Overs 16–20): Exposes every weakness. Bowlers with clever variations, pinpoint yorkers, and strong nerves are essential to prevent explosive finishes.

Only after understanding these phases can we identify where Punjab Kings consistently won or lost matches.
